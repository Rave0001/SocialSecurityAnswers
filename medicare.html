Medicare Basics - Your Guide to Coverage

Medicare is health insurance for people 65+, or under 65 with certain disabilities or conditions (like kidney failure). Here’s what you need to know in simple terms:

The Parts of Medicare Explained

  • Part A (Hospital Insurance): Covers hospital stays, skilled nursing, hospice, and some home health care. Free if you worked 10+ years paying Medicare taxes.
  • Part B (Medical Insurance): Covers doctor visits, outpatient care, and preventive services (like shots). Costs $185/month in 2025 for most people.
  • Part C (Medicare Advantage): A private plan option that bundles Part A, B, and often D. May add extras like vision or dental.
  • Part D (Drug Coverage): Helps pay for prescription drugs. You add it to Original Medicare or get it through Part C. Costs vary (~$40/month average).
  • Medigap (Supplemental): Extra insurance to cover costs Original Medicare doesn’t, like copays. Sold by private companies.

How Medicare Plans Work

You choose how to get your Medicare coverage:

  • Original Medicare (Parts A & B): Run by the government. You can go to any doctor or hospital that takes Medicare (most do). Add Part D for drugs or Medigap for extra help with costs. You pay deductibles (e.g., $240 for Part B in 2025) and 20% of some bills.
  • Medicare Advantage (Part C): Run by private companies approved by Medicare. Bundles A, B, and usually D. Often has lower out-of-pocket costs but you stick to a network of doctors. May include extras like hearing aids. Plans vary—some are $0 premium, others cost more.

Simple Tip: Original Medicare = more freedom, higher costs. Medicare Advantage = less freedom, often lower costs + extras.

Key Things to Know About Medicare

  • Sign Up: Start 3 months before you turn 65, or when you get disability benefits for 24 months. Miss it? You might pay penalties.
  • Costs: Part A is usually free; Part B has a premium. Advantage and Part D costs depend on the plan. Higher income = higher premiums.
  • Other Insurance: If you have job coverage, Medicare might pay second. Check with your employer!
  • Extras: Medicare doesn’t cover long-term care, most dental, or glasses. Some Advantage plans do.
